What Is ADHD?

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurological condition affecting focus, impulse control, emotional regulation, and executive functioning. It does not mean someone is lazy — their brain works differently.

"ADHD is not a lack of attention — it’s a struggle to control where attention goes."

Common ADHD Challenges

ADHD affects everyone differently, but common struggles include:

  • Difficulty focusing or staying on tasks
  • Mental overwhelm or feeling “scattered”
  • Poor time management / time blindness
  • Impulsivity
  • Forgetfulness and losing items
  • Emotional sensitivity and mood swings
  • Restlessness or hyperactivity
"It’s not that I can’t focus — it’s that I can’t choose what to focus on."

How ADHD Affects Daily Life

ADHD influences school, relationships, confidence, and routines. These challenges come from brain wiring, not personal failure.

  • Procrastination or difficulty starting tasks
  • Feeling misunderstood or judged
  • Trouble organizing belongings
  • Rejection sensitivity (RSD)
  • Struggling with consistency
  • Sleep issues

ADHD Strengths

  • Creativity and imagination
  • Hyperfocus on goals and passions
  • High energy and enthusiasm
  • Unique ideas and problem-solving
  • Deep empathy

How to Manage ADHD

  • Professional diagnosis & support
  • Therapy (CBT, ADHD coaching)
  • Medication (when prescribed)
  • Structured routines
  • Using planners, timers, reminders
  • Breaking tasks into small steps
  • Reducing distractions
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy sleep habits

Daily ADHD Tips

  • Use timers (Pomodoro)
  • Keep workspace simple
  • Try “body doubling”
  • Move often to reset focus
  • Write tasks immediately
  • Create consistent item locations
  • Reward small wins
